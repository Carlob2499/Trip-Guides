## What actually happened (skips, by driver)

**Heat (the dominant disruptor — both GO Fest days lost to it):**
- GO Fest Day 1 & Day 2 at EXPO Science Park — both skipped for **remote raids from the
  hotel room** instead. "Did not go to park... TOO HOT, little raids in-person." Same call
  both days — this wasn't a one-off, it was the standing decision once the heat hit.
- Bukchon Hanok Village — "too hot — too historical and no time."

**Rain (the second major disruptor):**
- Changdeokgung Secret Garden — skipped for rain; the group voted to go shopping instead.
- Cheonggyecheon walk — skipped; downpour was intense and they weren't dressed for it, so
  they sheltered at a Korean Fried Chicken place for chimaek instead.
- Freeform, verbatim on this: *"Despite umbrellas we were always wet. Shoes were often wet.
  Need better recommendations."*

**Distance beat the plan every time it competed with proximity:**
- Apgujeong shopping → skipped, did Myeongdong instead ("closer... convenience, as well as
  lunch").
- HYBE HQ → skipped, "far."
- Gobang Sikdang (Daejeon celebration grill) → skipped for being far; went to a KBBQ strip
  near I-Hotel instead, though "barely had room since everything closed by 11."
- This is the strongest confirmation yet of the low-commute-clustering pattern already in
  TRAVELER_PATTERNS — three independent skips, same reason, same trip.

**The Busan subway recommendation was NOT what actually happened:**
- The itinerary (carrying the in-Busan re-plot I incorporated 2026-07-16) recommends the
  subway Haeundae ⇄ Busan Station. The group took a **taxi** instead — "VERY CHEAP and
  direct." This is a real, reported contradiction of an idea that was never actually
  field-tested (it came from an unmerged branch written mid-trip, never seen live). See the
  flag to the user below — this needs a decision, not a silent edit.

**Transit / booking friction (freeform, summarized):**
- Uber substituted for Kakao T on the Daejeon Station → I-Hotel leg — bad experience
  (foreign-number registration, traffic, heat). Explicit ask: get a Korean e-SIM number so
  Kakao T actually works; translation support is limited without one.
- KTX VIP/first-class: cheap, but sells out fast — must book **days** ahead. Standing-room
  tickets exist but are "extremely uncomfortable and not even guaranteed."
- Buses were "frankly difficult" apart from the direct Limousine bus (6002, used successfully
  in place of the planned ICN T1 shower stop).
- Naver/Kakao Maps: "very not friendly, unlike Google Maps or word-of-mouth. Unreliable
  translation, navigation/GPS, needed multiple people to navigate."
- CatchTable "wasn't as good as I thought" — many places weren't listed on it or on any map.
- Tax-free: easier than expected, but inconsistent — some venues (Olive Young) do instant
  tax-free, others don't.
- T-money: convenience stores are everywhere for it, but Apple Pay T-money needs a **Korean**
  credit card; the physical card needs **cash** to top up.
- Airbnb: "annoying for the price for lack of AC... in summer, can be extremely contentious."
